On the very last day of class my Intro to Cognitive Science professor gave us his self-proclaimed "5 Keys to Happiness", and one of them was to spend your money on experiences rather than materials; so go take a trip somewhere. My family has always been fond of traveling, but it wasn't until that day in that classroom that I realized how much I truly enjoyed our various excursions. Whether it were the exotic cuisines or the unfamiliar streets, I always had a penchant for going to new places.

I saw these trips as opportunities. Opportunities to learn more about the world I inhabit with 7 billion others, opportunities to capture sights and sounds foreign to me, quickly, before I would leave and unlikely encounter them again, and opportunities to escape the bubble I'm in — the bubble that is UC Berkeley, the bubble that is privileged America — and realize that there is so, so much more out there.

This site isn't a blog and it isn't a photo gallery. The images and videos you see are more of a reminder for myself, and maybe for others who have come across similar paths, of moments and places perhaps otherwise forgotten. They're little mementos, each with their own larger stories.
